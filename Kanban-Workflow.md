# Agile Workflows & Kanban

## Research & Learning
* **How a Kanban Board Works:** A Kanban board visually maps out the workflow. It allows the team to see the exact status of every task at a glance, ensuring transparency without the need for constant status meetings.
[Image of a Kanban board with Backlog, In Progress, Review, and Done columns]
* **Board Columns:** * *Not Started / Backlog:* Tasks waiting to be picked up.
    * *In Progress:* Tasks currently being worked on.
    * *Ready for Review / QA:* Development is done; the task is waiting for my testing.
    * *Approved by Bot / Done:* Testing passed, and the task is completed.
* **Task Movement:** Tasks are "pulled" from left to right. The person actively working on the task is responsible for moving the card and updating its status with clear, written comments.
* **Benefits of WIP Limits:** Limiting Work in Progress (WIP) prevents context switching and burnout. It ensures we finish current tasks before starting new ones, maintaining a steady, manageable flow.
